MAIN PHOTOGRAPHER BIOGRAPHY

Brian Kenneth Somono's images don't simply record what's in front of the lens — they carry the weight of someone who knows what it means to truly stop and pay attention, because for a long time, he couldn't afford to.

Now published in TIME, Geo, and Terre Sauvage, Somono has built a body of work that spans continents and cultures, from the salt flats of the Danakil Depression to the river deltas of Southeast Asia. Each project is driven by the same instinct that saved him: slow down, look closer, stay longer.

He leads intimate field workshops capped at eight participants — not to teach technique first, but to teach attention first. It's the same lesson that changed his life: that the most powerful images, like the most powerful moments, come to those willing to stop and pay attention.
