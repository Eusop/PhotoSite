ABOUT Brian Somono

The Main Photographer

Drawn to Places
Rarely Witnessed

Brian Kenneth Somono didn't grow up with roots — he grew up moving. Different cities, different schools, different rules. For a stretch of his teenage years, loyalty meant something else entirely: he ran with a crew, and the streets were the only place that felt like home.

But life has a way of turning you around. A borrowed camera, a chance afternoon, and something clicked — not just the shutter. Photography gave Somono a reason to look at the world differently, to slow down instead of run, to witness instead of hide. What started as a way out became a calling.

Now published in TIME, Geo, and Terre Sauvage, he leads intimate field workshops built on the same lesson that changed his life — that the most powerful images, like the most powerful moments, come to those willing to stop and pay attention.
